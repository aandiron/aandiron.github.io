## Unseen Horror (draft: 2021-01-18)

When her eyes opened in the blackest part of the night, she found herself confused at first. She lay there staring into the dark for a few minutes, trying to sort the pleasures of her dreams from the sensory deprivation of this place she awoke in.

She was not fully awake, but reality had pushed itself into her consciousness enough for her to be aware she was in her dorm room. Even after midnight she would usually be able to hear music playing from someone's room. It was only in the very late hours that it got quiet in the dorms. It was quiet now, so it must be very late. Four or Five AM, probably. She could roll over and check the clock on the other side of the bed. But as soon as she had that thought, she realized that actually she couldn't bring herself to move.

That was strange. She had read for psychology class about sleep paralysis, and she initially thought this must be what she was experiencing. But she remembered that people with sleep paralysis aren't supposed to be able to control their breathing. She took a deep breath.

No problems there. She decides it's not sleep paralysis. She opened and closed her eyes. Seems like those are working too; though it's so dark it's hard to know for sure. She felt her bed move. Behind her. Just as if someone had moved to prop themselves up on an elbow --- to look at the person sleeping next to them in the bed.

The next day "Angel's Meltdown," as the girls called it, was the talk of the dorm. People cracked up all the time at school, but someone as stolid as Angel losing it was a worrying sign. Some of the other girls in the dorm saw the event as a reflection of their own lives --- if Angel couldn't hack it, how could they? More than one decided they needed to skip classes that day to preserve their emotional health.

The two Jennifers (Jennifer L. and Jennifer A. --- initially dismayed to find the automatic roommate selection had paired them with someone with the same name, but eventually becoming the best of friends) declared their room a mental health support zone, and left their door open all day. That this made their room the primary hub of dorm talk about the events of the night before was not something they considered a problem.

But for most of the morning even the Jennifers had pretty sketchy information. Most girls seemed to think Angel just couldn't handle the stress of her heavy courseload. A few said she had been attacked in the bathroom, but those stories were undermined by the girls who had seen her crying in the hallway. A thorough check of the bathroom and floor had been made, but Angel had clearly been alone.

Angel and Deanna had gone to their morning classes, so it wasn't until the afternoon when Angel was back in the dorm while Deanna had another class that the jennifers got some primary source information.
