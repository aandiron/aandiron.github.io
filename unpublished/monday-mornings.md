## Monday Mornings


Back when I worked at Harvard there was a professor named John
Stilgoe. He wrote a book called "Outside Lies Magic." At that time I
was a big believer in the critical perspective --- as I remain to this
day. And I think that is what initially I found so attractive about
Stilgoe. That dude was one cantankerous and critical old bastard. But
Stilgoe also had that thing going where he literally believed in
magic. And I was like "magic. psh. Give me science Stilgoe! Science is
where the critical perspective comes from."

Now though, I realize that Stilgoe arrived at magic _because_ of his
critical perspective on the world. The more you learn about science
and evidence-based knowledge, the more you realize how limited it is.
The world is so complex that science can only explain a tiny fraction
of what's going on. It's a matter of _faith_ on the part of scientists
that science is capable of explaining the huge part of existence that
they don't understand -- and that faith has consistently been tested
as the more we learn, the more we realize we don't know shit.

So over the last 20 years, _my_ belief in the critical perspective has
led me to where Stilgoe was 20 years ago (though without the
conservative-politics underpinnings). Now _I_ believe in magic.
There's a huge portion of existence that has _significance_ that just
isn't reflected in evidence-based data. The world is full of crazy
coincidence, statistics-defying events, unbelievable stories, and
UFOs. That shit _is_ magic.

And then Monday morning comes, and I have to go back to my boring-ass
job. And all the magic evaporates. Poof. Helped along by that
magic-killing machine, the computer, which I have to sit in front of
for the next 8 hours. The world turns cold and gray and dry and dull.
And the computer screen tries to trick you into believing it has some
magic with all its bright sharp colors. But it lies.

And my mind wanders off, and thinks about warm, wet genuinely-colorful
things, and bizarre stupid luck, and all the odd coincidences that are
the real engines of life -- all of which is encased in magic, not
science. And all of it feels pretty remote when I'm grinding out
office work. And I end up just sitting here on this Monday morning
wishing I could squeeze a little of that magic through the dumb
anti-magic tube of my computer. Just hoping for a little pea-sized bit
of magic that I could brush my existential molars with.
