I've always hated the term "being intimate." What an inappropriately delicate and saccharine way to describe the sweaty, smelly, sticky act of sex in all its physicality. Use any other term, please: bonking, boinking, boning, fucking, porking, hammering; anything that isn't so weak as "being intimate with your partner." It makes me sleepy just writing it.

But living with someone you love and want to be close to without having sex with them, I am finding, does actually seem to create this problem of a lack of intimacy. Unsurprisingly maybe, there's something about sex that's special. There is no other way to be so close to someone. You can try to spend more time cuddling or spooning, or other ways of just being physically near them. But that just isn't the same as having a big part of yourself literally _inside_ them. (Or, conversely, having a part of them inside you.) And then on top of that, you breathe their air, mix your bodily fluids with theirs, exchange pheromones, grind out your emotions, and just get to bask in their reassuring _desire_ for you. (If things are going well, anyway.)

Turns out, I actually miss being intimate.

Not that I think that closeness should be reserved just for your "partner" (another term of modern love that I hate). Pretty much anyone you have sex with should include at least an element of that closeness, I think. That's all for the good.

I suppose there might be some other ways to achieve an overpowering emotional intensity that might be akin to fucking. Like maybe if you murder someone together, or have some other secret you share that cannot be exposed beyond your little coupling. But then, even if you did have that, imagine how much hotter it would be if you were _also_ fucking your murdering partner? I suspect that was the enduring appeal of Bonnie and Clyde. Nobody said it out loud, but in the back of the heads of every person who read about them in the paper -- perhaps unconsciously -- they were thinking: damn, I'll be the sex is fucking _hot_ after you kill some police together.
